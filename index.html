How do Hindu non-profits spend your money?

by Rheaa Rao

Tax exemption laws have helped non-profit organizations in the U.S. grow into advocacy groups. This blurs ethical lines when religion-based non-profits use it for political lobbying.

Hindu non-profit organizations have rooted themselves in liberal politics in the United States and the conservative side back in India. The party in India whose ideas they're largely affiliated with are the BJP and RSS. These political parties propogate Hindutva or one strand of Hinduism. They have also been involved in the Gujurat riots in 2002 and are accused for human rights violations in India. Despite this, they are currently the party in power in India.

This project is a very tiny part of a larger project investigating the violations and affiliations of these non-profits
